Rules and Guidelines.

Debate Rules

General Rules >>

Words and Statements

Put simply; No mud slinging, no tantrum throwing.

Swear words and obscene statements are filtered in comments as you post them. If you do not see your comments , it must have been put up for moderation. Moderators regularly review comments, edit if suitable or delete if found unworthy. Word filters are update regularly to keep the site fresh and full of ideas. Words are also limited to 400 words, so that content does not become boring and bogus. Plagiarism

It is necessary to avoid copying content from elsewhere without reference. The comment section supports markdown for prettifying and ordering ideas.

Timing >>

Closed Debate

Debate starts at the stated time and may be modified by moderator due to reasons which may include unavailability of debaters. Debaters must have sought permission. Comments and opinions are open to all participating moderators and debaters when debate starts, closed to all others. Voting is enabled after debate ends and rebuttal is ongoing. Only registered followers can vote and votes cannot be retrieved or changed after being cast. Registered followers can then give opinions, rebuttals or seek clarification on points raised. Moderators close the debate when debate ends or at a preset time set.

Open Debates.

The same rules apply to open debates except that no judges are necessary and all community users are free to take sides and post their points. It does not limit posting to any group. Moderators still do their jobs and decide when voting ends.

Notifications >>

Logged in users can set notifications for each debate interested in following. Notifications are sent 15 minutes before commencement of debate to confirmed emails of followers. Your email provider may try to spam notifications from us, so add us to your address list or “mark as not spam”. Notifications are automatically sent to moderators, judges and debaters an hour before commencement of debate. For open debates, only moderators get notified. Regular notifications may be sent to update you on happenings and hot debates.

Judging >>

Judges access debaters as a group and on individual basis. Debaters are accessed on the following criteria;
  • Grammar/Vocabulary
  • Articulation/Enunciation
  • Brevity

Each of these are scored on a scale of 10 for each debater and adds up to the group’s average score of 30. N.B: Votes make up 70% (and may be increased for use case) of total points and may be the only judging criteria in open debates or debates excluding judges’ observation. Each judge gives his verdict (in his admin panel), collated and added to votes, to get the winner.

Votes >>

Votes is considered a major factor in winning debates since it takes up a larger chunk of the scores. Having a huge supporter base indicates the acceptance of your stated points by the community. Each debate section votes for the best debater.

Vote score = (votes for group/total no. of voters)x70

Total score = votes score + average judges score

Only registered users can vote and only opened during the voting period. It cannot be retrieved or changed on submission.

Badges and Rewards >>

Badges are awarded to users, debaters, judges, modrators and commenters for performing tasks or reaching a milestone. Go to badges to view all available badges. Sponsors are wanted. Send a mail to support[at]talkatif.com. Some badges may be necessary to explore some aspects of the community.

Suggesting Debates >>

The platform review all debates topic sent in. Endeavour to add a summary which include your reason for the topic or what it hopes to achieve. Be brief. The user who posted debate is automatically made a moderator and can add debaaters.

Rules are constantly being reviewed and updated to meet the standards of the community. Endeavor to keep checking back for changes.

Thank you for being a part of the community.

That’s all you need to know. Enjoy!!
